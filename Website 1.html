Enjoying Martial Arts

As I think about it more, I realize martial arts has been among my all-time favorite activities/hobbies/passions. It’s my best source of stress relief. I started as a kid, around 3rd grade, when I went to my first Karate dojo. Local instructors would come to my school and show us cool stuff to hook us into paying a large amount of money to learn how to make karate noises and kick good, with a dash of “stranger danger” training. Eventually my dojo closed, and I stopped for a few years. But I never stopped trying to learn new things, and after getting to college I found another outlet to continue martial arts. It reminded me how much I missed it.

Now let's discuss how to rise in the ranks with martial arts (for example Taekwondo)!

Tae Kwon Do Belt Ranks
White Orange Yellow Green
Purple* Blue Brown Red Black

Requirements to advance belt levels

  1. Learn the steps of certain forms; a step by step set of techniques to demonstrate technique
  2. Demonstrate increased proficiency in mold techniques and learn/improve on new techniques (kicks, punches, etc.)
  3. Show efficiency with self defense; how to handle confrontations and dangerous situations in life
  4. Combat prowess through sparring, which entails simulated fighting to train application of technique

The most important aspect of martial arts is beating people up being able to protect yourself appropriately and only as needed to keep yourself safe!

  • As I learned it, martial arts was not an invitation to go out and attack people indiscriminately. It was meant to give the tools to protect your life, and if necessary, the life of another. I also took my knowledge to competitions, in order to test my skills and earn trophies/medals.

I always believe that, as said by Rose Namajunas, Martial arts is about honor and respect.
